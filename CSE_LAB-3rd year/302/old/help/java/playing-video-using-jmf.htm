What kind of video are you trying to play? JMF is a pretty old library and won't be able to play most of modern video formats, only a few old ones (i am not even sure which ones).

Actually, if i am correct, to play something specific you will have to write/add your own video-encoders into JMF or atleast download and use existing ones, which are usually outdated.

If you really want to have something like tunable video player that could play any modern video there are two options (in my opinion):

  1. Use vlcj library to embed VLC video player into your Java-application

  2. USe JavaFX media player

I am offering only those two because i have digged through hundreds of libraries some time ago and there was nothing else even close to these two. Plus most of other libraries are outdated as well as JMF itself and these two are getting frequent updates and are supported with lots of users so any of those is the best choice.

If you don't mind including Java FX into your application - it might be the best choice, but its still not too stable. Next few updates might fix the situation though.

On the other hand - vlcj is stable and easily integrated into your Swing application (its not like its hard with Java FX, but its still nto as stable as i want it to be).

Anyway, choice is up to you.
